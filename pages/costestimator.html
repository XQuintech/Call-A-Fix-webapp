Common Washing Machine Faults and Errors

Before diving into repair services, it's essential to understand the common issues that can plague your washing machine:

  • No Power: This could be due to a faulty power cord, plug, or electrical outlet. Additionally, check for tripped circuit breakers or blown fuses that may prevent the machine from receiving power.
  • No Water Intake: A clogged water inlet valve or a kinked hose can cause this problem. Also, ensure that the water supply is turned on and that the filter screens in the hose connections are clean.
  • No Water Drainage: A blocked drainpipe or a malfunctioning pump can hinder drainage. Inspect the drain hose for clogs, and check the pump for any debris or obstructions that may impede its operation.
  • Machine Won't Spin: A faulty motor, belt, or drum bearing can lead to spinning issues. Additionally, the door lock mechanism may be malfunctioning, preventing the spin cycle from starting.
  • Excessive Vibration and Noise: Worn-out shock absorbers or unbalanced loads can cause excessive noise and vibration. Ensure that the machine is level and that the load is distributed evenly to minimize these issues.
  • Leaking Water: A damaged drum seal or a loose hose connection can result in water leaks. Inspect the door seal for wear and tear, and check the detergent drawer for clogs that could cause overflow.
  • Cycle Not Completing: The washing machine may stop mid-cycle due to a malfunctioning control board or sensor. This can also be triggered by power interruptions or unbalanced loads that prevent the cycle from progressing.
  • Unpleasant Odors: A buildup of detergent residue, mold, or mildew inside the drum can lead to foul smells. Regular cleaning of the drum and door seal, as well as running maintenance cycles, can help mitigate this.
  • Display Issues: Malfunctions in the control panel can cause the display to flicker or become unresponsive. This could be due to electrical issues or a fault in the control board itself.
  • Overheating: If the machine is overheating, it could be due to a faulty thermostat or a malfunctioning motor. This may also result from extended use without proper cooling periods.

Common Washing Machine Error Codes
General Error Codes:
  • E7: Overheating error (check for blocked vents or faulty thermostat)
  • E8: Low water pressure error (inspect water supply line and pressure regulator)
  • E9: Communication error (check wiring or control module connections)
  • E10: Load sensing error (verify load distribution in drum)
  • E11: Voltage error (check power supply and outlet)
  • E12: Door ajar error (ensure door is fully closed and latch is functioning)
Brand-Specific Error Codes:
LG:
  • PE: Pump error (check for blockages in pump or hoses)
  • CE: Communication error between control board and motor
  • dE: Door error (door may not be securely locked)
Samsung:
  • 3E: Motor error (check motor connections and brushes)
  • 6E: Heating error (inspect heating element and wiring)
  • dC: Door close error (check door latch and sensor)
Whirlpool:
  • F02: Slow drain error (inspect drain pump and filter for clogs)
  • F20: Water inlet error (check for valve issues or hose kinks)
  • F70: Communication error between control board and user interface
Bosch:
  • E15: Leak detected (check for leaks in hoses or internal components)
  • E24: Drain error (inspect drainage system for blockages)
  • E25: Filter blockage (clean or replace the filter)
Maytag:
  • F1: Control board error (replace or reset the control board)
  • F3: Temperature error (check temperature sensor and wiring)
  • F7: Drive motor error (inspect motor and connections)

What is Expected of a Washing Machine Owner

As a washing machine owner, you can take preventive measures to prolong your appliance's life:

  • Regular Cleaning: Clean the lint filter, detergent dispenser, and drum regularly.
  • Leveling: Ensure your washing machine is leveled to avoid excessive vibration.
  • Proper Loading: Avoid overloading the machine to prevent damage to the motor and drum.
  • Quality Detergent: Use high-quality detergent to minimize wear and tear on the machine.
  • Professional Maintenance: Consider scheduling professional maintenance checks every year.
Average Lifespan of a Washing Machine

The average lifespan of a washing machine in Nigeria is around 7-10 years. However, with proper care and maintenance, you can extend its life significantly.
